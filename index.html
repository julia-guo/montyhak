MontyHacks is located at Montgomery High School in Skillman, NJ.
about
Hosted by the MHS Computer Science Club and YECNJ, MontyHacks is the first hackathon at Montgomery High School and in Somerset County. We aim to expose more students to the exciting world of Computer Science.

This year, our theme is Hacking for Environmental Change. We hope to see some creative hacks regarding sustainability, resource conservation, renewable energy, etc. However, you don't have to make an environmental hack - we'll have other prize categories as well.

Beginners are welcome: we'll offer plentiful workshops, mentors, and tech resources to help you make the best hacks possible!
FAQ
What is a hackathon?
A hackathon is a coding competition, typically overnight, where students of various backgrounds team up to make innovative products (hacks).
Who can attend?
Anyone who is currently under 18 years old and/or enrolled in high school or middle school is able to attend.
How much does it cost?
Admission is free! However, we encourage you to bring a few dollars to donate to a non-profit of your choice such as GirlsWhoCode or the National Wildlife Federation. We'll have donation boxes set up, and you can choose one to donate to.
What should I bring?
Bring anything you need to make your hack - a laptop, mouse, drawing tablet, charger, etc. Also, bring a sleeping bag and a pillow if you plan on sleeping.
Do I need experience?
Nope! Beginners are welcome and encouraged to come; we'll have many workshops and mentors who will help you learn to make something great!
What's the selection process?
Applicants will be selected based upon their expressed interest, experience, etc. from their application. We will try to make sure that everyone who applies is able to go.
